Overview

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Financial Mathematics at Florida State University, working under the supervision of Dr. Lingjiong Zhu. My research focuses on stochastic optimization, the emergence of heavy tails in stochastic gradient methods, and the theoretical foundations of stability and generalization. Alongside theoretical work, I pursue applied projects in computational finance, including option pricing, risk modeling, and machine learning applications in financial prediction.

Research Interests

  • Optimization and Machine Learning Theory
  • Statistical Learning and Generalization
  • Stochastic Processes and Simulation Methods
  • Computational Finance and Derivatives Pricing
  • Quantitative Risk Management
  • Financial Engineering and Data Science Applications

Current Research Projects

  • Heavy-tailed Dynamics in Stochastic Gradient Descent with Momentum — analyzing tail indices and stability properties.
  • Decentralized SGD — investigating heavy-tail emergence and the effect of network geometry on optimization dynamics.
  • Option Pricing Engines — developing Monte Carlo, binomial/LSM, and PDE-based methods for European and American derivatives.
  • Credit Risk Modeling — building fairness-aware models for auto-loan decisioning with interpretability tools.
  • Deep Learning for Financial Prediction — applying LSTM architectures for stock price forecasting.
